Loop a few hundred items through an API at full speed and you'll trip its rate limit, get a wave of 429s, and lose half the batch. You don't need a queue service to fix it, n8n can pace itself.

## Space the calls out

Put a **Wait** node inside the loop so each item is spaced just far enough apart to stay under the per-second ceiling. When a `429` does come back, read the `Retry-After` header and wait that long before the next attempt instead of hammering blindly.

- Add a small wait between items to stay under the cap.
- Honor `Retry-After` on `429` responses.
- Back off exponentially, then give up after a sane ceiling so a broken upstream can't loop forever.

It's a few extra seconds per run in exchange for never dropping work, a trade worth making every time.
